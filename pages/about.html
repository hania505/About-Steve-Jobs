Early Life

Steve Jobs was born on February 24, 1955, in San Francisco, California. He was adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs. As a child, he showed interest in electronics and attended Homestead High School in Cupertino.

Education

Jobs enrolled at Reed College but dropped out after one semester. He continued to attend calligraphy classes and explored his interests in technology and design. His unconventional path later influenced Apple's product aesthetics.

Career

In 1976, Steve Jobs co-founded Apple Inc. with Steve Wozniak in his parents' garage. He was instrumental in launching revolutionary products like the Macintosh, iPod, iPhone, and iPad. After a brief departure from Apple, he returned in 1997 and led the company into a new era of innovation. He also acquired Pixar Animation Studios and played a major role in the entertainment industry.

“The people who are crazy enough to think they can change the world are the ones who do.” – Steve Jobs
